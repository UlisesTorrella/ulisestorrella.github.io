## Fair Termination for Resource-Aware Active Objects

Published at [APLAS 2025](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1007/978-981-95-3585-9_2) — also available on [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.15333).

We study the termination of concurrent programs written in a resource-aware active object language. Active objects are a model of concurrent computation where objects communicate via asynchronous method calls and execute tasks cooperatively. We consider a notion of fair termination, which guarantees termination under the assumption that every enabled task is eventually scheduled. We develop a type-and-effect system that infers resource bounds and uses them to prove fair termination.

## Análisis multiobjetivo sobre DTNs

[Master Thesis](https://rdu.unc.edu.ar/items/35e94a3b-4a31-4891-bcf5-abce3ce9f0e2) — Universidad Nacional de Córdoba, 2023.

Delay tolerant networks (DTNs) have been proposed as an architecture fit for deep space and interplanetary networking. Based on orbital dynamics, a contact plan can be known in advance to propose a competent scheduler. This thesis analyzes the modeling of DTNs with Markov decision processes (MDPs) to include energy consumption and delivery time as objectives. Applying multi-objective analysis using the STORM model checker, we expose underlying trade-offs of specific DTNs, using a Lunar Road Ring architecture as a proof of concept.
